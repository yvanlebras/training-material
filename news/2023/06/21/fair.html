ELIXIR-UK Fellow Launches New FAIR Data Management Training

Author(s) Katarzyna KamienieckaKrzysztof Poterlowicz
new topic new tutorial

Posted on: 21 June 2023 purlPURL: https://gxy.io/GTN:N00056

Katarzyna Kamieniecka (University of Bradford), a member of the ELIXIR-UK Fellowship Programme, has just launched a new series of lessons to improve Findable Accessible Interoperable Reusable (FAIR) data management in the life sciences. The lessons are designed to help researchers and research support staff understand the principles of FAIR data management and apply them to their own work.

The lessons cover three key topics:

  • FAIR in a nutshell: provides a short introduction to the FAIR principles and their origin.
  • FAIR data management solutions: discusses a FAIR data management framework that can be used to improve the findability, accessibility, interoperability, and reusability of research data.
  • FAIR Galaxy Training Material: shows how to set and use existing resources to make Galaxy training development and content creation FAIR.

The lessons are available online and can be accessed by anyone. They are a valuable resource for anyone who wants to improve their data management practices.

Where can I learn more?

You can find out more about the new lessons on the ELIXIR-UK Fellowship site and its recommended resources page. You can also follow Katarzyna Kamieniecka on Twitter for updates on her work.

More lessons coming soon!
